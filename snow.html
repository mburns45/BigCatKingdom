About the Snow Leopard

The snow leopard is one of the most rare big cats in the world. They can grow up to 7 feet long and reach up to 120 lbs. Although they are not the largest of the big cats, they are some of the most adaptable. The snow leopard is found in rough mountain terrian in Russia and China. In order to be more agile the snow leopard has shorter front legs and in order to maintain balance a long tail. To be able to traverse the cold rough mountains the leopard has large paws with fur underneath to protect them from this enviornment. Currently there are approximately 3,000 to 6,000 snow leopards spread across Europe and Asia.

Quick Facts

  • This big cat cannot roar
  • In nepal they hunt blue sheep
  • More related to tigers than leopards
  • Can jump as far as 50 feet depending on elevation/ location
