About The Cheetah

The cheetah is the fastest land animal in the world reaching speeds up to 80 mph. The cheetah is desgned for speed with its light body weight and small head. These characteristics make the cheetah more areodynamic and in order to maintain balance the cheetah uses its long tail like a counter balance to better manouver sharp turns. The cheetah body can get close to five feet long and a tail length of two and a half feet. The cheetahs weight can get up to 160 lbs, all of these attributes help the cheetah chase down prey like gazelles and impalas. There are approximately 6,500 cheetahs alive in the wild.

Quick Facts

  • Cannot roar but can meow and purr
  • Have around 2,000 spots
  • Can run 60 mph in 3.4 seconds
  • Can run 30-40 mph comfortably for half an hour
