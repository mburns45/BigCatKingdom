About The Bobcat

Commonly mistaken for a lynx the bobcat is the smaller one in the family. The bobcat can be identified from there small stuby tail and black strips on the tail and arms, also the bobcat doesn't have the long hair on there ears. The bobcat is one of the smallest of the big cats reaching lengths of 49 inches and weighing in at 40 lbs. The bobcat typically hunts rodents like mice, squirells, and rabits. The reason they have such small tails is because they hunt on the ground trying to catch small prey and dont want to alert them. There are approximately two to three and a half million bobcats alive in the wild today.

Quick Facts

  • Bobcats have a creepy scream that can be heard for miles
  • Male bobcat territory can span 30 square miles
  • Can jump up to 12 feet
  • Can kill animals up to eight times there size
