The Savannah

The savannah is the biggest biome in sothern Africa, it covers one third of the land there. The savannah with its great size is vast and can range from dry deserts to wet rain forest. With this comes some of the most beautiful animals and enviornment seen on Earth. The deserts here can reach low temperatures of 60 degrees and moderate heat with temperatures of 90 degrees. Depending on the wet or dry seasons the temperatures and climate can vary.

History of Africa

How has Africa's wildlife changed over time due to humans?

The history of Africa is vast with many up's and down's. Approximately 200,000 years ago the first humans had came from evolution to East Africa. During that time period humans hunted, gathered, and created civilizations. As a result of creating civilizations, Africas land was destroyed from deforestation and resource extraction. This has forced animals to move out of there natural habitats and live closer to humans than they would like. Because of poaching and the land being destroyed, there are many foundations and other non-profit organizations committed to rejuvinating the land.
