About The Mountain Lion

The mountain lion is the biggest cat in North America. They can reach lengths of up to eight feet long and weigh up to 220 lbs. The reason they are so big is because they hunt down very large prey like elk. More commonly the mountain lion will hunt deer and other smaller animals like rabbits, turkeys, and racoons. Mountain lions are often called other names like cougar, panther, and puma. There are currently around 20,000 to 40,000 mountain lions in the United States alone. There are more mountain lions in Canada and Centeral America.

Quick Facts

  • Mountain lions like to hunt at night
  • They can be found at sea level all the way to 10,000 ft in elevation
  • Can jump up to 18 feet vertically and 45 feet horizontally
  • Fourth largest big cat in the world
