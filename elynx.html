About The Eurasian Lynx

The eurasian lynx is on the smaller end of the big cats reaching lengths of two and a half feet long, with there weight reaching up to 65 lbs. The eurasian lynx can be typically found in mixed forests in Europe and open wooded regions in Asia, some also call the Himalayas there home. With the lynx size being so small they typically feed on baby deer or rabbits and hares, but they have been known to bring down prey much larger than there own size. There are approximately 50,000 eurasian lynx living in the wild.

Quick Facts

  • Third largest predator in Europe
  • Largest species of lynx in the world
  • Have killed deer up to 330 lbs
  • Was once on the brink of extinction
