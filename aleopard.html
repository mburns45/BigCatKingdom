About The Leopard

The african leopard is one of the strongest big cats in the animal kingdom. The leopard is able to carry 276 lbs straight up a tree, for scale that is about double there own body weight. A fully grown african leopard can get up to seven and a half feet long and weigh up to 200 pounds. The way an african leopard evolved it has shorter legs and a big head built for strength and power. The african leopard has a very strong neck in order to carry its prey up into trees so its meal isn't stolen from a hyena or a pact of african wild dogs. There are estimated to be 700,000 african leopards but there population is lacking in data.

Quick Facts

  • Is pound for pound the strongest big cat in the world
  • Smallest of the big cats in Africa
  • Can run 60 mph
  • Leopards are great swimmers
