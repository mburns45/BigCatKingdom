About The Ocelot

The ocelot is one of the most beautiful cats in the world. They have an exotic look and look similar to the cheetah and jaguar. The one main difference is the ocelot is much smaller. The length of an ocelot is between two and three feet, and weighing in at 34 pounds. Ocelot's will eat small prey like mice, frogs, birds, and fish. Although they are very small, they are the second largest spotted cat in South America falling quite short of the jaguar. Approximately there are 1.5 to 3 million wild ocelot's in South America.

Quick Facts

  • There have been Ocelot's spotted in the United States
  • Ocelot's are nocturnal
  • Teeth are designed not for chewing but for tearing and swallowing meat whole
  • Every ocelot's coat is unique to each individual
