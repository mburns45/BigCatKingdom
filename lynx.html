About The Lynx

The lynx is smaller in stature compared to the rest of the big cat family but is still a dangerous hunter. The lynx can grow up to two and a half feet long while weighing in at 24 lbs. On the lynx dinner menu varies from birds, hares, and small deer. Approximately 75% of there diet is hare although they are capable of hunting different prey. One characteristic that sticks out about the lynx is the ear hair, many scientist are unsure of why they have this with theories varying from to keep them warm to helping them hear. Estimates show there are around 50,000 lynx alive in the wild.

Quick Facts

  • If the snowshoe hare population drops so does the lynx
  • The lynx back legs are longer than the front for running and jumping
  • Only half of there offspring survive past one year
  • Lynx have natural snow shoes to help them traverse in the snow
