The Amazon Rainforest

The amazon rainforest is one of the most intresting places in the world. With thousands of animal species living there, and over 80,000 species of plants; The rainforest is detrimental to our everyday lives. Over 40,000 of those plant species play critical roles in regulating the global climate. Given the name of a rainforest it makes since why the amazon is always wet. The rain season spans from March to July, but rain will come all year. With the plants and founa being so vast you will see why the big cats that call this home have some of the most unique looks.

History of South America

How has South America and humans changed over time?

Humans have been dated back to living in South America no later than 16,000 years ago. During that time many civilizations have come and gone. More recently thanks to lidar (lazers that scan the earth from a plane) we have been able to newly uncover lost civilizations we have never known about before hand. From the lidar images they discovered Olmec and Maya structures that have been hidden away. What you can tell from the photos is humans have changed the landscape of where ever they go in any time period. Currently today 100,000 acres of the rainforest is destroyed each day. To give you an idea for scale that is approximately the size of California. This is a major issue that some are trying to combat but not enough support is happening. If we lose the rainforest many animals and plants will go extinct and in turn send a shockwave throughout the entire world.
