The Rainforests

The land in Asia and Europe is very large and has many different biomes. For simplicity we will only discuss the rainforests because of the big cats located there. The forest used to take up much more of a greater area in the past but due to deforestation much of that has been destroyed. The rainforests that have survived are some of the oldest forest in the World. The rainforest here also houses orangutans and elphants. Outside of Asia the only other place to find elephants is Africa. The only big cat that is not found in this biome is the snow leopard, more about the snow leopords biome will be disscussed on it's page found on the right.

History of Asia and Europe

How has Asia and Europe changed over time?

The history of Asia and Europe has many chapters. People first arived to Eurasia 50,000 years ago. There have been many dynasties in the past of Asia and Europe, China in 130 BCE under the Han Dynasty formally established the silk road (image depicted above). This important trade route linked Eurasia together. Eurasia in the 18th century then split into Asia and Europe. Thus the wildlife across the now border is very similar. Only when you get to the high Ural mountains in Russia or the tropical rainforests in Indonesia do you see completly different flora and fauna.
